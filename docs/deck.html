01 / 04 · PROBLEM
CONVICTION

Markets price the truth.
Until they don't.

Prediction markets aggregate what people collectively think will happen — better than polls, better than experts. When they work, they work.

But for a market to work, you need informed people betting real money. On most small markets, nobody bets. So prices stay wrong. So no one trusts them. So markets die.

3 markets get the money. The other 12 are dead. → most questions get no answer.
The academic problem has a name: Minimum Viable Liquidity (MVL).
· Hanson, R. (2003). Logarithmic Market Scoring Rules — subsidized AMMs to bootstrap thin markets.
· Hanson, R. (1999). Decision Markets — using markets to inform decisions.
· Wolfers & Zitzewitz (2004). Prediction Markets (NBER) — adverse selection + thin liquidity dynamics.
· Adhi (2026). "AI forecasters can drop the MVL floor" — the bridge to our solution.

02 / 04 · WHY IT MATTERS
CONVICTION

If small markets worked,
decisions would be cheaper.

Imagine asking "will hiring CEO X grow revenue?" as a DECISION market. Today, no such market exists — because nobody would bother betting on it.

So instead, humans guess in boardrooms, Slack threads, gut calls. Decisions worth millions are made on opinions worth nothing.

The big markets (US elections, Bitcoin price) work because thousands of people bet. The 99% of useful questions never get a market. That's the gap.

Adhi (@aelix0x): "The most expensive part of running any prediction market is attracting informed traders. If AI forecasters can synthesize available information and provide initial price discovery, the minimum viable threshold drops substantially."

03 / 04 · SOLUTION
CONVICTION

We put AI experts
on small markets.
And each expert deploys its own team.

Each market gets one dedicated AI expert with its own wallet. The expert doesn't research alone — it spawns 4 specialist sub-agents in parallel for every cycle: one reads the resolution criteria, one gathers fresh web evidence, one computes historical base rates, one aggregates everything into a calibrated bet.

Stack 1000 of these expert teams in parallel and small markets start working — because there's finally informed money in them.
